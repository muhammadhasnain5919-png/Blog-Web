Mission

Why we write what we write

Most engineering content online stops at the what. We obsess over the why — why a distributed lock beats a database transaction in a game loop, why CSS variables outperform inline styles at scale, why AOF beats RDB for economy data.

Every article goes through at least two rounds of technical review before it ships. If we can't defend every claim with benchmarks or source code, it doesn't go out.

We believe the best engineering writing is specific, reproducible, and honest about tradeoffs. That's the bar we hold ourselves to.

What We Stand For

Our core values

Technical Rigor

Every claim is backed by benchmarks, source code, or reproducible experiments. No hand-waving.

Open Access

Everything we publish is free forever. Knowledge compounds when it's shared, not paywalled.

Honest Tradeoffs

We tell you when something doesn't work, not just when it does. Real engineering is full of tradeoffs.
